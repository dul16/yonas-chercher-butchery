Gored-Gored
  • It is made from cubed tenderloin or ground steak.
  • It is dipped in to awaze or mitmita sauce, which gives it a spicy taste.
  • Its richly layered seasoning can be searingly spicy or pleasantly warm.
  • Depending on personal choice it can be served raw or lightly.

Kurt (Tire-Siga)
  • While Gored-Gored is already cut into cubes when served, Kurt is not sliced into tiny cubes when served rather it is personaly cut by the hand of the diner.
  • A large part of the meal is the ritual of cutting the meat. The act isknown as q'wirt,mean "to cut" in Amharic.
  • Like Gored-Gored it is served a spicy sauces.

Dulet
  • Dulet is made from minced meat, mixed with various spices, onions, and somtimes offal.
  • The spices such as cumin and coriander gives the dish a rich and aromatic flavors.
  • The minced meat is cooked toa tender consistency, often combined with sauteed onions for added sweetness.

Mlas-Senber
  • It typically features lentils cooked with a blend of spices.
  • The lentils are often cooked until tender, resulting in a hearty and comforting texture
  • Like most Ethiopian dishes, it is served with injera allowing diners to scoop up the lentils and enjoy a communaldining experience

Gomen-Besiga
  • This dish promarily features collard greens (gomens) cooked with tender piece of beef
  • The dish has rich savory flavor, enhanced by the spices and the natural sweetnessofsauteed onions.
  • It can be served with other dishes or by itself in different occasions.

Shekla-Tibs

It is a classic Ethiopian dish, a flavorful and aromatic stir-fry that bursts with a medley of spices and textures. It features tender strips of beef cooked in afragrant blend of spices,including berbere, a signature Ethiopian spice blend known forits fiery heat. Its bold flavors and beautiful presentation make it a true celebration of Ethiopian culinary traditions.

Kitfo

Kitfo, a staple in Ethiopian cuisine, it is a minced raw beef dish, marinated in mitmita (a chill powder-based spice blend) and niter kibbeh (a clarified butter infused with herbs and spices). It is often served alongside ayibe (mild cheese) or cooked greens known as Gomen. Kitfo is served with injera or kocho which is a thick flatbread made from enset plant.
